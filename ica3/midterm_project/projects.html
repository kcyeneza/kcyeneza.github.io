PROJECTS

GLOWSTER

The glow-ster was my final project in OBJECT, worked on with a fellow classmate, Riley Meere. In this project, we were intruiged by the restaurant industry and ways to improve the experience. From here, we designed a coaster that changes colors overtime, depending on how recent the drink has been picked up. using ADAFRUIT Playgrounds, touch sensors, neopixels, all programmed in C. Riley designed the outer shell, and 3D printed the casing.


COLOR-GARDEN

Color garden was a simple project that uses tissue paper, folded into flowers and neopixels, strictly for aesthetics. Coded in C, the lights change color and the tissue paper is both functional and design-based to diffuse the light and make the flowers appear as if they were glowing.


SCARY-RECYCLED JELLYFISH

This jellyfish was conceptualized on using multiple inputs and outputs. A potentiometer and a photolight sensor were both used to adjust the pitch of the screaming jellyfish and the led color change. When the potentiometer knob was turned, the sound would change pitch, and when the photolight sensor reacted to different lights, the led's would either change color or remain the same. With this project, I also wanted to focus on the encasing, so I used a clear acrylic fruit container, as well as recycled trash bags to make the jellyfish-like shape. All programmed in C.
